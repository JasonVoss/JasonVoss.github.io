Coronavirus infections in the United States began spreading in the year 2020. The virus was declared a pandemic by the CDC on March 21. The virus did not begin in all areas simultaneously. The rate of infection shows how the virus affected different regions of the country at different times. In the early months of the pandemic tests were scarce. It is presumed that there was a population who recovered who were never diagnosed. Those that were severely ill were more likely to be tested. This causes mortality to decrease over time, as more tests become available. Widespread vaccinations did not begin until 2021.
